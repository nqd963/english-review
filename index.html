However, the current wave of AI-driven automation differs in important ways. Unlike previous revolutions, which primarily affected manual and routine cognitive tasks, AI is increasingly capable of performing complex analytical and creative work. This has led to debates about whether education systems need fundamental reform to prepare students for a future where adaptability and continuous learning will be more valuable than static knowledge. Some experts argue that universal basic income may eventually become necessary as a response to widespread automation.
